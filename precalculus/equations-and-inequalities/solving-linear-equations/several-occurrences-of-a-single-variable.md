# Several occurrences of a single variable

## Fractional forms with binomial numerators

### General Process

1. Multiply both sides of the equation by the Least Common Denominator (LCD).
2. Use distributive property, $$a(b+c)=ab+ac$$, to cancel out the denominator and expand the equation.
3. Solve for variable.

### Example Problems

#### Example

Solve for $$v$$. Simplify your answer as much as possible.

$$
\frac{v-2}{7}=11-\frac{5v-5}{4}
$$

**Step 1: Multiply both sides of the equation by the Least Common Denominator (LCD).**

First, we need to find the LCD of the given fractions $$\frac{v-2}{7}$$ and $$\frac{5v-5}{4}$$. This means we need to find the least common multiple (LCM) of the denominators $$4$$ and $$7$$, which is $$28$$.

With the LCD identified, we can multiply both sides of the equation by the LCD $$28$$, giving us the following equation:

$$
28(\frac{v-2}{7}) = 28(11-\frac{5v-5}{4})
$$

**Step 2: Use distributive property to cancel out the denominator and expand the equation.**

We can use distributive property to expand the left-hand-side from $$28(11-\frac{5v-5}{4})$$ to $$28(11)+28(-\frac{5v-5}{4})$$.

$$
28(\frac{v-2}{7}) = 28(11)+28(-\frac{5v-5}{4})
$$
